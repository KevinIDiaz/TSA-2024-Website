Geothermal

Introduction

With the use of geothermal pumps, the energy consumption of a household decreases, and corresponding emissions are also reduced. Compared with air-source heat pumps, emissions can be reduced by up to 44 percent and up to 72 percent compared to standard air-conditioning equipment. These pumps can last over 20 years and are easy to install. Another positive about geothermal pumps is that they have no condensing units like air conditioners. The prices for geothermal heat pumps can range from $800 to over $49,000 depending on location, type of pump, and how many pumps are purchased.
