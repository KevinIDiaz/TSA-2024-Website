Solar

Introduction

A photovoltaic panel, also known as a solar panel, absorbs sunlight with a PV cell and transforms solar energy into electricity. These cells are strung together, which creates a module. One solar panel usually has 30 modules. There are two types of solar panels: industrial and residential. Industrial panels are traditionally larger, making them more efficient. Although less power is generated, it is generally easier to install a residential one. Color also affects the solar panels. The standard colors are black and blue, which is what most industrial and residential panels use. Solar panels' costs can range from $4,500 to $36,000. The cost can depend on location and company but usually costs around $16,000. Although it may seem expensive, eligible solar systems bought through 2032 will receive a 30% federal solar tax credit, which will reduce the cost of the solar panels.
