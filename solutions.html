Geothermal

Geothermal heat pumps are one of many great ways to help the environment. It uses the ground to transfer heat to and from a home. Since ground temperatures are much more consistent compared to air temperatures. Throughout most of the United States, the upper 10 feet of ground stays at a temperature of 45 -75 degrees while air temperature can range from 0 to 100 degrees. This allows geothermal heat pumps to be two to three times more efficient.
